Nighthawks

They scissor edges of twilight, cutting black shapes into sky. The wet silver of quick wings open against eternity, as if to erase an end with a beginning.
a note Four lines in which the birds become a kind of editorial mark on the sky. "As if to erase an end with a beginning" — that's the whole consolation, in one line.
