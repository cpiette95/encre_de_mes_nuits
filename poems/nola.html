NOLA

August 25, 2025
At night, we played partners in crime on Bourbon Street, A Voodoo Daiquiri on the table, your hands around my waist. Loud laughs and smiles, a jazz piano playing half-dead. The next day, we stopped beneath the haunted live oaks Their white hanging catkins like farewell hankies in the wind. Meaningless tatters ready to dissolve our memories in the air We spent a few hours wandering inside the bayous. Their meandering waters, bearing no beginning, no end. Unlike our story, which from the start bore the shadow of its own end. Our last walk led us through NOLA's cemetery. Now we belong too to those proud tombs that guard the never-returning. Had I known, I would have laid flowers in tribute to that time.
