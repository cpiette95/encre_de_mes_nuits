Swing

January 14, 2026
Today I got on a wooden swing In search of the inexhaustible journey Of a little girl's afternoon in the playground. There I found myself, an immobile traveller. Amid the cheerful cries and laughter My hands started sweating. As if seasick, on a ropeless wobble board I struggled against a vertiginous hurricane Ripping apart that little girl inside me Sitting right there in the eye of the storm. I opened my eyes again. There was no storm, not even the slightest wind. Only the usual: time quietly passing. And I, stiff and silent, on that swing Secretly contemplating a different journey — What would happen if I stopped holding the chains?
· · ·

Context & Notes

The poem reflects on an afternoon spent with Y. in a playground in Somerville, where sitting back on a swing awakens memories of childhood and the painful awareness of the impossibility of going backwards in time.

Seeking to reconnect with the innocent and carefree experience of the children, I become confronted with an inner struggle, ending with thoughts of suicide.
